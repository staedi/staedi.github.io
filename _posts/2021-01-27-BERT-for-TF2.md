If you are in the world of NLP, it's highly likely that you've stumbled upon WordVec or BERT, short for Bidirectional Encoder Representations for Transformers by Google. BERT is the state-of-art model for machines to understand human language. Like WordVec, it hopes to understand contexts of texts.
While it's top of the league for the purpose, one is developed with Tensorflow 1.x in mind. 

The world has changed and Tensorflow 2.x has taken place although many practitioners are still forced to used 1.x version or compatible modules in 2.x.

The original team who developed BERT at Google hasn't upgraded it up to Tensorflow 2.x.
However, Tensorflow team did implement BERT model to work with Tensorflow 2.x despite a few changes of the parameters.

Folks who are interested can visit `tensorflow/models` [Github](https://github.com/tensorflow/models) of Tensorflow team.
